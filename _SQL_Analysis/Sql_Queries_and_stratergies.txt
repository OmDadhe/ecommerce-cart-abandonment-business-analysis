E-Commerce Cart Abandonment & Sales Performance Assessment

Objective

The organization is experiencing concerns regarding declining sales and a high cart abandonment rate. The objective of this SQL analysis is to:

• Validate revenue performance trends
• Assess customer retention behavior
• Identify cancellation and checkout friction indicators
• Detect operational bottlenecks impacting satisfaction

Although the dataset does not include explicit cart logs, abandonment has been inferred using behavioral proxies such as cancellation rates and post-purchase dissatisfaction.
Dataset used: Olist brazillian ecommerse dataset Kaggle


2. Queries Used

A. Revenue & Sales Health Analysis

Query 1: Monthly Revenue Trend

Business Objective

To determine whether revenue decline exists and identify seasonal or structural contraction patterns.

SQL Used

WITH monthly_data AS (
    SELECT
        DATE_TRUNC(DATE(o.order_purchase_timestamp), MONTH) AS order_month,
        SUM(p.payment_value) AS monthly_revenue
    FROM `olist-ba-project.ecommerse_analysis.orders` o
    JOIN `olist-ba-project.ecommerse_analysis.payments` p
        ON o.order_id = p.order_id
    WHERE o.order_status = 'delivered'
    GROUP BY order_month
)
SELECT
    order_month,
    monthly_revenue,
    LAG(monthly_revenue) OVER (ORDER BY order_month) AS prev_revenue,
    ROUND(
        SAFE_DIVIDE(
            monthly_revenue - LAG(monthly_revenue) OVER (ORDER BY order_month),
            LAG(monthly_revenue) OVER (ORDER BY order_month)
        ) * 100,
        2
    ) AS growth_percent
FROM monthly_data
ORDER BY order_month;


Key Findings

• Revenue peaked in November 2017 at ~1.15M
• Growth remained positive through most of 2017
• 2018 shows moderate volatility but no structural collapse
• No sustained multi-month decline observed

B. Customer Behavior Analysis

Query 2: New vs Repeat Customers

WITH customer_orders AS (
    SELECT
        customer_id,
        COUNT(order_id) AS total_orders
    FROM `olist-ba-project.ecommerse_analysis.orders`
    WHERE order_status = 'delivered'
    GROUP BY customer_id
)
SELECT
    COUNT(CASE WHEN total_orders = 1 THEN 1 END) AS new_customers,
    COUNT(CASE WHEN total_orders > 1 THEN 1 END) AS repeat_customers,
    ROUND(
        COUNT(CASE WHEN total_orders > 1 THEN 1 END)
        / COUNT(*) * 100,
        2
    ) AS repeat_customer_rate_percent
FROM customer_orders;

C. Cart Abandonment Proxy Analysis

Query 3: Cancellation Rate

SELECT
    COUNT(order_id) AS total_orders,
    COUNT(CASE WHEN order_status = 'canceled' THEN 1 END) AS cancelled_orders,
    ROUND(
        COUNT(CASE WHEN order_status = 'canceled' THEN 1 END)
        / COUNT(order_id) * 100,
        2
    ) AS cancellation_rate_percent
FROM `olist-ba-project.ecommerse_analysis.orders`;

D. Operational Impact on Customer Satisfaction

Query 4: Delivery Time vs Review Score

SELECT
    r.review_score,
    ROUND(
        AVG(
            TIMESTAMP_DIFF(
                o.order_delivered_customer_date,
                o.order_purchase_timestamp,
                DAY
            )
        ),
        2
    ) AS average_delivery_days
FROM `olist-ba-project.ecommerse_analysis.orders` o
JOIN `olist-ba-project.ecommerse_analysis.reviews` r
    ON o.order_id = r.order_id
WHERE o.order_status = 'delivered'
AND o.order_delivered_customer_date IS NOT NULL
GROUP BY r.review_score
ORDER BY r.review_score;

| ------------ | ----------------- |
| 1            | 20.85 days        |
| 5            | 10.22 days        |

Interpretation

Customers giving 1-star reviews experienced delivery times nearly twice as long as 5-star customers.

This clearly indicates logistics performance is a major driver of customer dissatisfaction.

Delivery delay is likely a key contributor to reduced repeat behavior and perceived abandonment.

Strategic Recommendations (Aligned to Scenario)

Although the observed cancellation rate (0.63%) is low, the analysis reveals structural risks related to customer retention and delivery performance. These risks, if left unaddressed, can gradually contribute to increased abandonment perception and revenue instability. The following recommendations are preventive, data-driven, and operationally actionable.


1. Reduce Delivery Cycle Time to Improve Customer Retention

Data Basis

Customers who gave a 1-star review experienced an average delivery time of 20.85 days, whereas customers giving 5-star reviews received their orders in 10.22 days on average. This indicates that dissatisfied customers waited approximately twice as long for delivery.

Why This Works

Delivery time has a direct and measurable relationship with customer satisfaction. Extended delivery cycles reduce perceived reliability and increase the likelihood of negative reviews, refunds, and reduced repeat purchase probability. Since repeat purchasing stabilizes long-term revenue, improving logistics performance directly influences retention and lifetime value.

Reducing delivery time improves:

* Customer satisfaction scores
* Trust in the platform
* Repurchase probability
* Brand credibility

Recommended Actions

* Implement seller-level Service Level Agreement (SLA) benchmarks for dispatch and delivery time.
* Introduce performance-based ranking in search results prioritizing faster sellers.
* Establish regional warehousing or inventory pooling for high-demand categories.
* Monitor average dispatch delay and penalize sellers exceeding defined thresholds.

Expected Business Impact

Improving delivery performance reduces dissatisfaction-driven churn and increases the probability of repeat transactions. This creates revenue stability even if acquisition costs rise.


2. Strengthen Customer Retention Strategy

Data Basis

The dataset indicates negligible measurable repeat purchasing behavior using transaction-linked identifiers. This suggests that revenue growth is heavily dependent on new customer acquisition.

Why This Works

Relying primarily on new customer acquisition increases marketing costs and revenue volatility. Retention reduces acquisition dependency and increases profitability. Industry benchmarks indicate that even a 5% increase in retention can significantly improve long-term profitability.

Improving retention:

* Stabilizes revenue
* Reduces marketing cost pressure
* Increases customer lifetime value
* Decreases abandonment perception over time

Recommended Actions

* Introduce a structured loyalty program tied to order completion and review participation.
* Implement automated post-delivery engagement campaigns within 3–5 days of order completion.
* Use category-based personalized product recommendations.
* Create reactivation campaigns targeting customers inactive for 30 days.

Expected Business Impact

Improved repeat purchasing reduces revenue volatility and strengthens lifetime value metrics, mitigating long-term abandonment risk.


3. Enhance Checkout Monitoring and Payment Experience

Data Basis

Although the cancellation rate is relatively low (0.63%), cancelled-after-approval cases indicate that some customers drop off after initiating checkout.

Why This Works

Checkout friction compounds at scale. Minor issues such as delayed confirmation, unclear freight charges, or payment validation failures reduce conversion efficiency. Even a marginal improvement in checkout conversion can materially increase total revenue.

Improving checkout transparency:

* Reduces transaction hesitation
* Improves trust perception
* Increases conversion rate
* Reduces late-stage cancellations

Recommended Actions

* Display total payable amount (including freight) early in the checkout process.
* Implement real-time payment failure tracking dashboards.
* Reduce payment confirmation latency.
* Add trust indicators and secure payment messaging during checkout.

Expected Business Impact

A small percentage increase in checkout completion can produce significant incremental revenue due to the scale of transactions.


4. Implement Seller Performance Governance

Data Basis

Delivery time is strongly correlated with review scores. This implies variability in seller performance directly affects customer satisfaction.

Why This Works

In marketplace-based eCommerce models, platform reputation depends on seller logistics quality. Underperforming sellers can disproportionately damage customer perception and increase silent churn.

Improving seller governance:

* Improves delivery consistency
* Enhances review scores
* Reduces dissatisfaction-driven abandonment
* Strengthens platform credibility

Recommended Actions

* Develop a seller performance scorecard including average delivery time and review rating.
* Introduce tier-based visibility (e.g., Preferred Seller, Fast Dispatch Seller).
* Penalize sellers exceeding delivery time thresholds.
* Provide logistics performance analytics dashboards to sellers.

Expected Business Impact

Improved seller consistency leads to higher average satisfaction and increased retention probability.


5. Proactive Risk Segmentation and Service Recovery

Data Basis

Customers experiencing longer delivery times are more likely to give low review scores. These customers represent high churn risk.

Why This Works

Service recovery is more cost-effective than acquiring new customers. Identifying at-risk customers before churn occurs reduces abandonment perception and protects lifetime value.

Targeting dissatisfied segments:

* Prevents silent churn
* Improves brand recovery
* Increases repeat purchase probability
* Reduces long-term abandonment risk

Recommended Actions

* Identify customers with delivery time greater than 15 days.
* Segment customers who provided review scores of 1 or 2.
* Offer apology vouchers or targeted discount incentives.
* Implement a structured feedback recovery program.

Expected Business Impact

Proactive intervention reduces churn probability and improves long-term revenue sustainability.



Strategic Positioning Summary

While the current cancellation rate does not indicate a severe abandonment crisis, operational inefficiencies—particularly extended delivery times and weak retention signals—pose structural risks to long-term growth. By strengthening logistics governance, enhancing retention strategy, and improving checkout transparency, the organization can mitigate future abandonment escalation and stabilize revenue performance.
